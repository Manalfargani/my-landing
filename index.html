Master Storytellers

Sarah J. Maas

Sarah J. Maas is the #1 New York Times and internationally bestselling author of the Throne of Glass series and A Court of Thorns and Roses saga. Known for her powerful storytelling and strong, relatable characters, Maas's works have become a staple in the fantasy genre.

Holly Black

Holly Black is renowned for her captivating stories woven with dark magic and enchanting worlds. Her most famous works include The Spiderwick Chronicles and The Folk of the Air series, which have both garnered a large fan base and critical acclaim.

J.K. Rowling

J.K. Rowling, best known for her Harry Potter series, has touched millions with her unique blend of magic and humanity. Her books have been translated into over eighty languages and have brought the joy of reading to countless readers worldwide.

Brandon Sanderson

Brandon Sanderson is a prolific contemporary fantasy author known for his Mistborn series and his role in completing Robert Jordan's epic Wheel of Time series. His works are praised for their intricate systems of magic and deep world-building.

Neil Gaiman

Neil Gaiman's work transcends genre boundaries. Known for titles like American Gods and Coraline, Gaiman combines elements of folklore, horror, and fantasy, creating stories that are both critically acclaimed and beloved by readers of all ages.
